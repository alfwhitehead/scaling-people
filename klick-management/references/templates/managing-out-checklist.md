# Managing Out Checklist

*Mirror of the book's "Managing Out Checklist" (Chapter 5 exercises and templates). Work through these steps in order. This checklist covers performance-based departures; see the `plan-departure` skill for gross misconduct and layoff guidance.*

---

## Phase 1 — Documentation and feedback

- [ ] Provide feedback on performance issues — in person, in a regular 1:1, not as a surprise.
- [ ] Document the feedback in writing after each significant conversation (see pip-template.md Stage A and Stage B email templates).
- [ ] State explicitly — in at least one documented conversation — that the employee is not meeting the expectations of the role.
- [ ] Give the employee a clear opportunity to course-correct; document the agreed-upon next steps.

---

## Phase 2 — Formal process (if applicable)

- [ ] If one-off feedback has become a persistent pattern without improvement, assess whether a formal performance improvement plan (PIP) is needed.
- [ ] Draft the PIP using the pip-template.md; review with HR before presenting.
- [ ] Present the PIP to the employee; document their acknowledgment.
- [ ] Conduct weekly PIP check-ins; document progress at each check-in.
- [ ] At the PIP end date, assess outcome: back on track, insufficient progress, or role move.

---

## Phase 3 — Pre-work before the departure conversation

- [ ] Assemble all written feedback on performance issues and, if relevant, prior PIP and results.
- [ ] Familiarize yourself with local employment laws (protected classes, discrimination law, termination processes, required notice periods or severance obligations).
- [ ] Inform your manager that you are proceeding to a departure conversation.
- [ ] Inform your HR partner; confirm they are aligned on the decision and aware of any legal considerations.
- [ ] Confirm severance matrix or any applicable company policy with HR.
- [ ] Prepare for the conversation: know what you will say, what the logistics options are, and how you will respond to the most likely reactions.

---

## Phase 4 — The departure conversation

- [ ] Have the conversation in person if at all possible (or on video for remote employees — not by phone or email).
- [ ] For junior-level employees: summarize the PIP / performance history; ask how they think things have gone; state clearly that it won't work out; move quickly to departure logistics.
- [ ] For senior-level employees: discuss the performance gap in terms of this specific role and company; allow 2-3 conversations if needed; move to transition planning once there is alignment.
- [ ] Move from the decision to logistics as quickly as possible: when will their last day be, how do they want to inform people, how can you help?
- [ ] If the employee mentions "retaliation" or "discrimination": pause the conversation and involve HR and legal before continuing.
- [ ] Complete the conversation in no more than 1-2 sittings for junior roles; allow for 2-3 conversations over a short period for senior roles.

---

## Phase 5 — Severance, legal, and logistics

- [ ] Confirm severance package and terms with HR / legal; communicate to the employee.
- [ ] Confirm the employee's last day and any handover or transition period.
- [ ] Arrange offboarding logistics with IT, HR, and facilities (access revocation, equipment return, final paycheck timing).
- [ ] If equity vesting dates are relevant, ensure they are addressed in the departure terms.

---

## Phase 6 — Team communication

- [ ] Agree with the employee on the communications plan: what will be said, who will say it, and when.
- [ ] Announce the departure to the team or organization. Keep messaging factual and respectful — do not disclose performance details.
- [ ] If the role requires immediate coverage, communicate the transition plan at the same time as the departure announcement.
- [ ] Thank the departing employee appropriately for their contributions if circumstances allow.

---

## Phase 7 — Post-departure follow-up

- [ ] Monitor team morale in the days and weeks following the departure; check in with key team members individually.
- [ ] If the departure was a significant one, consider a retrospective: what did you learn about hiring, onboarding, or performance management that you would do differently?
- [ ] Begin the process of backfilling the role if needed.

---
