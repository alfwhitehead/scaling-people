## Motivators to address

*Most people are primarily motivated by recognition of impact, not purely financial gain. Frame compensation as an indicator of how the company views their contributions.*

**What primarily motivates this person — impact recognition, financial reward, promotion trajectory, something else?**

## Expected reactions and how to handle them

**If receiving a raise / promotion — how to celebrate it:**
*(Reinforce the impact and behaviors that earned it. Don't set the expectation that the same increase will repeat automatically.)*

**If no change — anticipated reaction and response:**
*(Own the outcome. Don't apologize or make promises. Tie to performance or program design. Remind them when the next opportunity is.)*

**If disappointed despite a meaningful increase — how to respond:**
*(Help them understand the system; give context on where they sit relative to peers if possible. Reframe: "How can we work together so that in six months, your compensation and perceived impact feel more aligned?")*

**If they bring up a comparison to a colleague's pay:**
*(Stay focused on this person. "I can't discuss anyone else's compensation, but if you think something is unfair, I'm happy to make sure HR looks into it.")*

## Conversation script outline

**Opening — check for understanding of the philosophy:**
- "Have you reviewed our comp philosophy? Let me walk you through it..."
- Answer questions; commit to following up on anything you can't answer now.

**Describe the outcome:**
- Remind them of their current compensation.
- Share the base salary for the coming period. If there's been an increase, celebrate it — name what they're being recognized for. If no change, reference the program design and, where appropriate, tie to performance.
- Share the bonus target, if applicable.

**Open for discussion:**
- Create space for questions.
- Move with the conversation — congratulations, working through concerns, etc.
- Close by reinforcing that you value their contributions.
