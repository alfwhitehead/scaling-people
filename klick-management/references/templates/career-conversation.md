## Pre-Conversation

### Manager prep

Before the conversation, reflect on what you already know about this person:

- What career decisions have they made that you understand well?
- What motivations or values are still opaque to you?
- Is there a decision or assignment coming up where understanding their arc would be directly useful?
- Have you had a previous career conversation? If so, what were the development goals from that conversation?

### Reminder to send to the report (1 day before)

> Thanks for taking the time to walk me through a bit of your life history and career in our session tomorrow. Mostly, I want to get to know you better. I'd like to learn about the choices you've made so far and what motivated you to make them. Feel free to share about where you grew up, your school experience, and why you chose the path you did. We'll focus most of the time on the career steps you've taken and why. If useful, bring a copy of your résumé.
>
> Toward the end of the conversation, I'd love to project out five years and have you think about where you see yourself in your career — not a specific title, but the kind of work you'd be doing and who you'd be working with. No one is held to any five-year plan; it's a helpful reference point as we continue working together.

## Conversation

*Walk-through-history approach. The most important thing: actively listen, take notes, and ask "why" often. You are trying to understand what motivates this person and why they made the choices they did.*

### Section 1 — Origins

**Prompt:** "Tell me about where you grew up."

Notes:
*Probe on: family context, early interests, where they went to school, why they chose that school. Don't pry on personal topics; stay open if they want to share. Most people don't mind giving location, schooling, and early interests.*

---

### Section 2 — School

**Prompt:** "Tell me about where you went to school and what you studied."

Notes:
*Probe on: why they chose their school (if applicable), why they chose their course of study, what interested them, what didn't.*

---

### Section 3 — Career history

**Prompt:** "Walk me through your career — what did you do after school, and why did you make each move?"

Notes:
*Probe on: why they chose each role, what they were good at and enjoyed, what was harder and why, what they learned. Surface patterns. Key questions:*
- *"What was your favorite job? Your least favorite? Why?"*
- *"What did you think you would do when you started out? Have you ended up there?"*
- *"What decisions are you proud of? Any you'd make differently?"*

---

### Section 4 — Current role

**Prompt:** "What drew you to this role, and what are you finding most energizing or challenging about it so far?"

Notes:
*Probe on: alignment between their motivations and the current work, any emerging skill gaps, whether they feel over- or under-challenged.*

---

### Section 5 — Future vision

**Prompt:** "What sort of work do you see yourself doing in the future? Who will you be working with? What will your day look like?"

Notes:
*Probe on work type (management, research, execution, communication, leadership, deep technical work) rather than specific titles or roles. What experiences or skills would move them toward that vision? What have they not yet explored that they want to?*

---

### Wrap-up

Recap the conversation aloud with the person. Share any patterns or themes you noticed. Invite them to react or correct your read.
