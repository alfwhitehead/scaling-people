## Management style

- **Collaborative vs directive:** [How you tend to make decisions — do you want to talk it out and see options, or do you move to a call quickly? What does this mean for how people should bring you a decision?]
- **Hands-on vs hands-off:** [When you get into the details and when you stay out. Note any conditions — e.g., you're more hands-on when something is new, or when you're worried things are off track.]
- **How I give feedback:** [Your approach — how timely, how direct, what format, what you expect in return.]
- **How I receive feedback:** [What you want people to do when they have a concern about you or the way you work. Make it easy for them to say so.]
- **How I make decisions:** [Your default process. Do you need data, time to think, a conversation, a set of options? What's the best way to get a decision out of you quickly when one is needed?]
- **Watch-outs:** [Where you tend to go wrong — the failure modes of your strengths. Be specific. This is the most useful section.]
