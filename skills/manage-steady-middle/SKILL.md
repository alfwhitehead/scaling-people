# Manage Steady Middle

The steady middle is the part of your team that is easy to neglect — they're not demanding your attention and they're not struggling. But they are often your greatest source of stability and cultural continuity, and neglecting them has quiet, compounding costs. This skill helps you think about your medium performers as a portfolio to be actively managed, not a baseline to be taken for granted.

Part of `feedback-and-performance` (Chapter 5 of *Scaling People*).

## Rubric

1. **Don't neglect the steady middle.** Medium performers are easy to deprioritize because they're neither on fire nor requiring rescue. That's a mistake. Many are culture carriers and sources of institutional stability — especially on teams where work has a consistent, repeatable shape. Neglect leads to quiet disengagement.

2. **Match the team context to what steady performers can offer.** Not every team needs constant change and stretch. A team that delivers consistent, well-executed work in a predictable cycle may be better suited to steady performers than one that needs high-growth innovation. Steady performers in the right context are not a compromise — they're the right hire.

3. **Every person in the talent portfolio needs active management.** Assess each steady performer: What would it take to support them better, coach them toward higher performance, or help them find a more suitable role or team? Each person should have a development plan, even if the plan is "sustain and recognize."

4. **Make it okay to reach a terminal level.** Some people will not be promoted beyond a certain point. Provided they're contributing well, allow for that stasis. Seek their feedback on how to improve the team. Take time to celebrate their consistent performance — which may look less like comp rewards and more like verbal recognition and special assignments.

5. **Recognition for steady performers looks different.** Steady performers are less likely to push for raises or titles. Verbal recognition, inclusion in meaningful work, and explicit acknowledgment of what they contribute to the team's culture and stability often matter more to them than formal rewards. Name it specifically.

6. **Look for underexplored potential.** "Medium performer" is a designation in context, not a permanent label. It's also about the right team context: someone who looks average on your team might be excellent elsewhere, and some steady performers haven't yet had the opportunity to show what they can do with more challenge or a different kind of work. Stay curious.

## Draft-mode intake

1. "Who is the steady performer you're thinking about, and what does 'steady' look like for them specifically?"
2. "What's the team context — is this a high-change environment or a more consistent execution environment?"
3. "Does this person have a current development plan? What are they working on?"
4. "Have they reached or are they nearing a terminal level for this role? How have you talked about that?"
5. "What's the most concrete thing they need from you right now — recognition, a new challenge, more direction?"
